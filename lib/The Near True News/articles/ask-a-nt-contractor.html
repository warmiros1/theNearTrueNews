Ask a NT Contractor

December 9, 2014

By: Tamara Hearst

Dear NT contractor,
I’m currently in a relationship again with my ex. What advice do you have for the second time around?
Sincerely,
Richard Weller

Hey Rich,
For starters, you’ve gotta be ready to rebuild your relationship. Building a relationship needs constant work. Just like my work at the school, it never really seems to be over. The three most important things you need in a relationship are listening skills, a hard hat, and worker boots. Trust is the foundation of any relationship. Also concrete. Lots of concrete.

Hey NT contractor,
I really like this girl, and I want to ask her out in a romantic way. What should I do?
Thanks,
Robert Pots

Bobster,
Surprise her with romantic gestures. Instead of serenading her with a boombox outside her window like that geeky Evanston grad John Cusack did, you should just wake her up with the sweet, manly song of my people: the jack hammer. The best time to do this or any construction work is about 5:30 AM.

Hi New Trier contractor,
I’m a freshman this year, and I just got a new girlfriend. Do you have any tips for easing into the dating scene?
I look forward to your response,
James Buckner

Hi Slim-Jim,
Fashion is everything. Neon orange is so in this fall. Ill-fitting neon vests are always best and are nicely paired with jeans that are just a little too low. If your girlfriend walks by you and your buddies in the rotunda, cat call and whistle at her really loudly. Trust me, girls love it. If she has asbestos, leave her. Make sure that she shows you the asbestos-screening results so you KNOW FOR A FACT that she is all clear. If not, you may wake up with asbestos, yourself. Finally, the size of the hammer DOES matter. You still need to wield plenty of power behind it, but that floor will only be properly nailed with a substantial hammer.
