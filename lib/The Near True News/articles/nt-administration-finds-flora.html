New Trier Administration Finds Flora Growing out of Floorboards after IGSS Lock-in

October 21, 2014

By: William Armiros

Photo by: Tor Camorlinga

New Trier's administration was distraught but not all too surprised when they found a variety of trees and shrubbery growing out of the floorboards in the second floor rotunda. Coming only days after the IGSS lock-in, wherein all IGSS students spend the night at New Trier, this phenomenon was not particularly shocking to school administrators.

Experts agree that these students simply could not live with being deprived from nature for such an extended period of time, so they used their connection with Earth and Gaea herself to turn the rotunda into a veritable biosphere. Administrator Susan Phillips commented, “We have come to expect this type of collateral damage from the IGSS lock-in, the IGSS bake sale, and every period IGSS meets. All four of them. But we embrace it because we know it’s the best alternative education these kids can get.”

IGSS students could not be reached for comment on the subject, likely because they are currently hiding in the trees.
