Ask a Glenbrook South Student

August 22, 2014

By: The Near True Staff

In order to get a glimpse into the otherwise estranged lives of our neighbors to the West, the Near True News petitioned the GBS administration for a peek into their lives.

What kind of fun apps do you download on your iPads?
For the last time, we have chromebooks. We do not have iPads. I can not believe New Trier bought students iPads instead of Chromebooks. Chromebooks are on the frontier of technology allowing us to do anything your outdated web browser can do. You want to play chess? BAM. Go to freechessonline.net. You want to check your email? BAM. Go to gmail.com. You want to play Clash of Clans? Well you can’t actually do that on a chromebook, but you can watch videos of it on youtube.com. Your ipad does not have a full keyboard and a trackpad, does it? I thought not. Think about that the next time you use an “app” to do something. I have the power of the internet at my fingertips, what do you have?

What are your favorite things to do near the lake?
If you open Google Maps on your Chromebook (it should be bookmarked), you will see that we are approximately eight miles to Lake Michigan. That’s not that much to walk in my knockoff Sperries. Now that’s a great feature of a chromebook. Additionally, you can download these things called “webapps.” Webapps are great! They can do so much! Sometimes they sit there on your computer, sometimes they provide you with useful tools. You can even download a webapp that creates a “friend.” The internet says he is virtual, but he is so real to me. His name is Stan. If I forget to feed Stan using Chromo-food, he gets sick and goes to the Chrome-clinic. Stan is great. Stan actually loves the lake, he just doesn’t how to swim.

Are you guys like smart or something?
Yes we are smart. My Chromebook can load up Answers.Yahoo.com in a matter of seconds. In fact, the other day, I asked a question about my… er… infection. Yeah, Chromebooks are great. They really allow you to see the web, the way it should be seen.
