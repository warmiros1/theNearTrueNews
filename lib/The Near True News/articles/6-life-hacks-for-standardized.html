6 Life Hacks for Standardized Tests

September 3, 2014

By: William Armiros

For this year’s juniors, standardized tests of all varieties are just around the corner. Here’s a few tips to get your dream score.

1. Stop time - Time can be one of the biggest obstacles to doing well on the ACT, but if you master the ability to freeze time at will then you’ll have no problem!

2. Be like Lucy and use 100% of your brain - Sometimes during the ACT you just lock up and can’t remember any of your tutoring. One way to avoid this is to be like Lucy from the recent sci-fi thriller and use your entire brain capacity. With that simple trick your forgetfulness will be a thing of the past!

3. Kidnap and torture an ACT test maker until they tell you the answers - Nobody ever said the ACT was a fair game, so there is no reason you shouldn’t abduct and by means of waterboarding or methodically cutting off fingers, force an ACT test designer to tell you all the answers on the upcoming test. This is a great example of using all resources available to you!

4. Work out a system with your fellow test-takers - The ACT and SAT are a great place to make friends! So go ahead, mingle before the test starts and plan out an extremely convoluted system to communicate answers to each other during the test.

5. Fake a seizure during the test - Nothing stirs things up during a standardized test like the classic fake seizure halfway through. If this just wasn’t your day and you don’t want this to be scored, just fall out of your chair and start convulsing on the ground. Extra points if you can foam at the mouth!

6. Just imagine the test in its underpants - This simple and time-tested approach helps calm your nerves right before a the big test. Just picture that thick test book in its tighty-whities and you’ll realize it’s not that intimidating after all.
