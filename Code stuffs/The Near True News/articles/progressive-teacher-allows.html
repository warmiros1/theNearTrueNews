Progressive Teacher Allows Students to Choose how to Fail Tests

September 15, 2014

By: Will Armiros

Ms. Camley, an AP European History teacher, is breaking traditional educational boundaries this year with her new, progressive approach to testing. She believes that instead of the authority figure, the students should be allowed to decide how they fail their tests. This move is totally unprecedented and the largest step towards alternative learning since the inception of IGSS.

Camley herself describes the process: “I believe in options for our young adults. So I’m offering to test stuff they never learned, give them tests that are too long, or grade with unparalleled intolerance. I find this approach really lets the students have a different perspective on tests and American education in general.” Some students are even engineering never-before-seen ways to fail tests!

Students have reached a generally positive consensus on the policy. Senior Claire Thompson said, “I love Ms. Camley’s teaching method. I never blame her when I do poorly on a test because it was obviously our class’s fault!”

Other teachers have announced plans to follow suit as soon as next semester. Administrator Gretchen Stevens was thrilled about this prospective policy. “First the iPads and now this! Before you know it, we’ll be so progressive our freshmen will be taking AP tests on their Apple Watches.”

At press time, Camley was collecting a pile of half-finished tests from students kicking themselves for choosing the “not enough time” option.
