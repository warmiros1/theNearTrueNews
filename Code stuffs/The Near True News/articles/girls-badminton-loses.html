Girls Badminton Loses Handle on Shuttlecock in Loose Match

September 21, 2014

By: Matt Leszczynski

When the leaves that line Winnetka avenue start to turn, every New Trier student has one thing on their mind: girls' badminton. Ever since the historic match of Lee vs. Chung back in '89, New Trier students have been avid badminton fans, or as they like to call themselves, "shuttlecock lovers." The shuttlecock lovers were unfortunately deflated by the first badminton match of the season.

New Trier played the unranked Hoopeston Cornjerkers (IL) at home in a match which many predicted the Trevs winning. Unfortunately for the Trevians, the match quickly turned sour. The first game of the day put New Trier's Steinberg against Hoopeston's Kochburn. Kochburn won in straight sets, and racked up five aces. Steinberg said in an interview, "The court was pretty slippery, and it was pretty tough getting enough coverage on the shuttlecock." As the shuttlecock continued to rise and fall throughout the day, so too did the woes for the Trevs.

The second match had red-shirt senior Sarah Lang versus Veronica Rubright in her first match of the season. Before the match Lang said she was, "eager to go out and whack some shuttlecock." Alas the eagerness to whack shuttlecock didn't translate to a win for lang. The Cornjerkers' Rubright consistently beat Lang on her backhand side.

Despit losing the second set, Rubright ended up winning the final set without losing a single point. Lang was plagued with wood shot after wood shot, strokes where the tip of the shuttlecock rubbed off the frame of the racquet. After the game Rubright said, "The key is the red tip of the shuttlecock - consistently making contact with that red tip is the best way to take control of a match."

After the Cornjerkers winning the rest of the matches with relative ease, the shuttlecock lovers' worse nightmare came true - losing the home opener. Thankfully the Cornjerkers handled their victory with grace, and captain Woodburn released a statement: "This victory has been an incredible experience for us Cornjerkers, and these matches really tested our endurance." Woodburn added that the team was bursting with excitement and hopes to see the Trevians in the IHSA state finals.
