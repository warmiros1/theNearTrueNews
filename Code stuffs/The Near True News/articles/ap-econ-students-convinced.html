AP Econ students convinced they’ve learned enough to balance the US budget

May 14, 2015

By: William Armiros

Photo by: Tor Camorlinga

Right after finishing up the fifth unit of their first year economic studies class, AP Economics students came to a startling discovery. After browsing articles about the state of our economy, these students found out that they could totally apply their skills to balance the budget of the US government.

Senior James Yern commented on the state of affairs: “Wow, if we just decrease the supply of guns and armaments, the resulting surplus will let us break even easily. This is unit three stuff. Honestly, I’m surprised no one else has seen this before.”

Several economics students quickly banded together to make a petition on Change.org titled “CONY 2015 - Change Our Nation’s economY.” A main idea behind the petition is to “eliminate all costs.” It has picked up a lot of support, receiving over 10,000 signatures from econ students across the country.

AP economics teacher Ms. Garfield was astounded by the work her students were doing. Garfield said, “It’s powerful stuff. You know, these kids are real geniuses because they spend five months learning about something and are automatically experts on it. I don’t know why I never thought of shifting the entire country’s demand curve to the right!”

At press time, CONY 2015 was writing a letter to their congressman, outlining as complex of a plan as their first-year knowledge would allow.
