Glencoe School Installs Space Program for Science Classes

August 24, 2014

By: William Armiros

In a completely unprecedented decision, the board of Glencoe’s Central School allocated roughly $2 billion towards a brand new space program for its students. This decision shocked the nine thousand residents of glencoe, just as the wildly successful movie theater wing project did the year before.

There was growing debate over whether the board would pick this space shuttle program or its alternative, the roller coaster referendum. However, despite it being at the upper end of the budget, they eventually went with the space program. Mr. Dillard, one of the board members, felt “the students will really get a rich experience from this. Some schools send kids to the zoo, we send kids to the moon. It’s all about expanding horizons.” When asked about the costs, Dillard laughed saying, “Are you kidding? We’re Glencoe!” He then turned around and did a Scrooge-McDuck-esque dive into a room literally filled with gold coins.

Sources confirmed that the educational benefits to the preposterously expensive program are without comparison. Mr. Jaffe, a science teacher at Central, says “students will conduct cutting-edge experiments, like the effect of 0-gravity on a vinegar and baking soda volcano.”

The plan is to send several missions into the vast expanses of outer space starting with the class pet, Fluffy the hairless Siamese cat. After orbiting Earth a couple of times, Fluffy should safely land in Lake Michigan. This, administrators confirmed, ought to kill two birds with one stone because it is positively impossible to get Fluffy to take a bath, the silly girl!

The best part of this program is that everyone in Glencoe public schools will be included. The valiant eighth graders will don their space gear only days before receiving their diplomas to have this rare educational experience. The fifth graders will play a huge part in engineering the rockets thrusters and motherboard. Even the kindergarteners will help out by practicing their countdown skills. All in all, this program perfectly symbolizes what Glencoe is about: fabulous wealth, ridiculous displays of said wealth, and having fun.
